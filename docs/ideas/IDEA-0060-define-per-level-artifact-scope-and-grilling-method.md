# IDEA-0060: Define per-level artifact scope and grilling method

## The Idea

Captured verbatim at SES-0085: "I would like to point out that Business Goals are the highest level of abstraction in the Groundwork paradigm. They only track original intention and what is to be achieved and say nothing about how it will be achieved or implementation details. So architect consultation doesn't make sense here. I would like to create an IDEA so we can follow up in the future on clearly defining the scope for each of the artifacts and how they should be grilled."

## Spark Context

Raised at SES-0085 round-1 prep, during the IDEA-0058 take-up rechartering BG-0002. The facilitator had proposed the discretionary system-architect consultation at Business Goal level; the stakeholder declined it on the principle that Business Goals are pure-intent artifacts — the highest abstraction level in the Groundwork paradigm, tracking what is to be achieved and never how. The follow-up work is to define, for each artifact level (Business Goal, Epic, Story, Spike, Component Doc), its scope boundary — what belongs at that level and what is out of place — and how grilling should be conducted at each level (question banks, consultation requirements, abstraction discipline). The existing DEC-0292 through DEC-0296 consultation rules and the goal-grilling question bank are prior art this take-up should reconcile with.
